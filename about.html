About the Author

Ricky Finch is a PhD candidate in the Texts & Technology program at the University of Central Florida. His research sits at the intersection of digital humanities, interactive narrative, and pedagogical design.

His dissertation examines Twine's Chapbook story format as a platform for teaching coding concepts through interactive fiction. Using platform studies methodology, his work analyzes how Chapbook's design shapes both the creation and reception of interactive narrative—and how that design can be harnessed in the classroom.

Shopping Mall 1999 is the centerpiece of that research: a game built to teach five foundational coding skills through the experience of exploring an abandoned mall, guided by a cat named Oliver.

Originally from Nashville, Ricky holds a BA in English from Lipscomb University and an MA in Composition, Rhetoric, and Digital Media from Nova Southeastern University. He likes Oreos, has a small obsession with Rosie the Riveter, and is directionally challenged. (Where is south, really?)
